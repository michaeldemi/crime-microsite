Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common ways burglars enter homes in our area?
According to police reports and security studies, the vast majority of residential break-ins are crimes of opportunity. Intruders almost always target the weakest and most accessible points of a home, with ground-floor windows and glass patio doors being the top two entry points. They rely on being able to break the glass quickly and quietly to gain entry in under a minute.
How does security film compare to an alarm system or security bars?
The best security is layered. While alarm systems are great for alerting you after someone is already inside, security film is a proactive measure that helps prevent entry in the first place. Unlike security bars, which are effective but can make a home feel like a prison, our security film is virtually invisible. It offers a powerful layer of protection that doesn't change the look of your home.
How exactly does the film stop someone from breaking in?
It's important to know that security film doesn't make glass "bulletproof." Instead, its power lies in a special, heavy-duty adhesive that bonds the film to the glass. When an intruder strikes the window, the glass will crack, but the film holds all the pieces firmly together in the frame. This makes it incredibly difficult and time-consuming to create an opening. The sustained noise and effort required to get through the film is a powerful deterrent, forcing most intruders to flee.
Is the film noticeable? Will it make my windows dark or bubbly?
Not at all. Modern security films are manufactured to be optically clear, meaning they are virtually invisible once installed by a professional. There is no tint, haze, or bubbling. As an added benefit, the film also blocks up to 99% of harmful UV rays, which helps protect your furniture, floors, and artwork from fading over time.
